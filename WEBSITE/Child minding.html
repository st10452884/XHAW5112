Course Overview:

Caring for young children is a rewarding but challenging tasks that requires specific skills and knowledge. Our Child Minding Course provides practical training to equip you with skills needed to care children from birth through toddlerhood. Learn how to meet the unique needs of chidren at different developmental stages to ensure their well-being and growth.

Course Content:

  1. Birth to Six-Month-Old Baby Needs
    • Feeding and Nutrition: Understand the basics of breastfeeding, bottlefeeding, and introducing solid food.
    • Sleep and Rest: Learn about sleep patterns and safe sleep practices for newborns
    • Hygiene and Diapering: Master techniques for bathing, changing diapers, and maintaining hygiene.
    • Safety and Handling: Gain confidence in handling, soothing, and ensuring the safety of young babies.
  2. Seven-Month to One-Year-Old Baby Needs
    • Introducing Solid foods: Learn how to safely introduce a variety of solid foods and manage feeding schedules.
    • Encouraging Motor Skills: Understand activities that promote crawling, sitting up, and standing.
    • Sleep Transition: Adapt to changing sleep patterns and introduce a bedtime routine.
    • Early Communication: Learn ways to encourage early language development, including babbling and simple gestures.
  3. Toddler Needs
    • Nutrition and Meal Planning: Know the basics of balanced meals and healthy snacks for toddlers
    • Potty Training: Gain tips and techniques for starting and managing the potty-training process.
    • Behavior Management: Learn strategies for handling tantrums, setting boundaries, and positive reinforcement.
    • Encouraging Independence: Promote skills like dressing, feeding, and simple chores to build self-confidence.
  4. Educational Toys
    • Choosing Age-Appropriate Toys: Learn to select toys that match a child's developmental stage.
    • Promoting Learning Through Play: Understand how toys can encourage cognitive, motor, and social skills.
    • Safety Consideration: Identify safe materials and designs for children's toys.
    • Using Everyday Items for Play: Discover how to use common household items to create engaging and educational play activities.
