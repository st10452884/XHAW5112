Course Overview:

We give learners the fundamental knowledge and abilities needed to handle most medical situations in our Comprehensive First Aid course. Being able to offer immediate help in any situation—at home, at work, or outside—can make all the difference in saving a life. This training ensures that you are equipped to confidently and skilfully handle emergencies by covering a wide range of topics.

Course Content:

  1. Wounds and Bleeding
    • Acquire knowledge on how to treat cuts, abrasions, and punctures, as well as more serious injuries.
    • Know the correct procedures for applying direct pressure, elevating the affected area, and applying bandaging to reduce bleeding.
    • Understand the symptoms of shock and how to handle it properly.
  2. Burns
    • Understand the differences between thermal, chemical, and electrical burns.
    • Understand how to properly treat burns that are first, second, or third degree.
    • Recognize how to prevent infection, protect the affected area, and cool burns.
  3. Fractures and Sprains
    • Learn how to identify and treat sprains, fractures, and dislocations.
    • To stop additional harm, learn how to immobilize wounded limbs.
    • Know when to use splints and how to tend to the injured individual while you wait for help to arrive.
  4. Emergency Scene Manangement
    • Learn the importance of priotizing care based on the severity of injuries.
    • Gain confidence in assessing an emergency scene safely and efficiently.
    • Understand how to call for hep and communicate effectivey with emergency services.
  5. Cardio-Pulmonary Resuscitation(CPR)
    • Learn the lifesaving skills of CPR for adults, children, and infants.
    • Practice proper techques for chest compressions and rescue breaths.
    • Understand when and how to use an automated external defibrillator(AED)
  6. Respiratory Distress(Choking, blocked airways)
    • Learn to recognize the signs of respiratory distress, including choking and blocked airways.
    • Understand how to perform the Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts) and back blows.
    • Learn the steps for providing care to individuals who are unconscious due to airway obstructions.
